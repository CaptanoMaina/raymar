Mt Longonot

Believed to have last erupted in the 1960s, Mt longonot is a stratovolcano in the Rift valley region in Kenya. One of its unique features is the thick forest that lies within the mountain's crater. Its rim provides great a great scenery of the Rift Valley. You also get to see wildl animals as you hike through the mountain.A 3.1 km trail runs from the park entrance up to the crater rim. Going up the mountain takes two hours and going round the crate takes another two hours.

Aberdare Region

Aberdare Forest

The Aberdare the third highest range of mountain Kenya, with altitudes ranging from 7,000 to 14,000 feet above sea level. Its thick vegetation and prolific game has made it the favorite destination of travelers who appreciate the outdoors and wildlife, since the 1950s. The thick forest is home to a variety of species. The Aberdares has two ‘Tree Hotels’, unique safari lodges set high in the canopy above waterholes and natural salt licks.An Aberdares forest experience is unforgettable. Activities include; Full day Safari Drive in the Aberdare Game Reserve, Morning Safari Drive to the Solio Game Ranch, Bird Watching,Horse Riding, Hiking,Nature Walk, Spa activity, Sundowners/Bush dining, Full day visit to Ol Pajeta and view the Chimps.

Mt Kenya

Mt Kenya, climbing 5 199 meters, is Africa's second highest mountain and Kenya’s tallest. The range is regarded as the realm of Ngai, god of the local Kikuyu people. The scenery surrounding this world-heritage site is breathtaking. The scenery surrounding this designated World Heritage Site is breath-taking. It is pristine wilderness with lakes, tarns, glaciers, dense forest, mineral springs and a selection of rare and endangered species of wildlife. Experience Africa’s second largest peak and ‘’touch the sky’’. Activities include Hiking, Camping, Caving, Nature Walks

Watamu

Enjoy the white-sands and soft breeze coming off at the beach at Watamu. Watamu is a magical slice of coastline and includes its own marine national park where you can see the rich and diverse bird life, fish, turtles and dugongs. It is an excellent location at which you can enjoy the nearby Crede ruins, Arabuko Sosoke Forest Reserve and the mangrove waterways in the Mida Creek. Other activities include: Mida Creek boardwalk and canoe ride, Sudi Island Mangrove Forest canoe ride, Day trip to Watamu Marine, Glass-Bottom Safari ride.
